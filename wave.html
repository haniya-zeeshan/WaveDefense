Safety Guide

Flood Precautions

Select a phase to see what you should do. Knowing this can save your life and your family's life.

📻

Stay Informed

Monitor NDMA alerts, local radio, and weather apps. Sign up for SMS warnings in your area.

🗺️

Know Your Zone

Find out if your home is in a flood-prone area. Know the nearest high ground and evacuation route.

📄

Secure Documents

Put CNICs, property papers, and passports in a waterproof bag. Keep digital copies on your phone.

🔌

Power Safety

Know how to turn off electricity, gas, and water at the main switch. Practice it with your family.

🏠

Protect Your Home

Move valuables to upper floors. Use sandbags at doors. Clear gutters and drains near your house.

👨‍👩‍👧

Family Plan

Decide a meeting point if you get separated. Share your plan with neighbours and relatives.

🏃

Evacuate Immediately

If authorities say leave — LEAVE. Do not wait. Take your emergency kit and go to higher ground.

🚫

Never Walk in Floodwater

Even 15cm of fast water can knock you down. Water hides open drains, broken roads, and wires.

🚗

Avoid Driving

Most flood deaths happen in vehicles. If your car stalls in water, leave it and move to high ground.

⚡

Stay Away from Power Lines

Flooded areas may have live wires. Never touch electrical equipment if you are wet or standing in water.

📱

Save Battery

Keep calls short. Use text/WhatsApp. Charge your phone as soon as power is available.

🆘

Call for Help

Contact your family members if you are in danger.

🏚️

Check Before Entering

Do not enter your home until authorities say it is safe. Check for structural damage, gas leaks, and unstable walls.

💧

Water Safety

Do not drink tap water until confirmed safe. Use bottled water or boil for at least 1 minute before drinking.

🧹

Clean Carefully

Wear gloves and boots when cleaning. Floodwater carries sewage and bacteria. Disinfect all surfaces.

🦟

Prevent Disease

Standing water breeds mosquitoes. Use repellent, drain all stagnant water, and watch for cholera/dengue symptoms.

📸

Document Damage

Take photos of all damage before cleaning for insurance or government compensation claims.

🧠

Mental Health

Floods are traumatic. Talk to family, community, or a counsellor. It is okay to ask for help.

Pack this Emergency Kit before flood season. Keep it near your exit. 🎒

💧3 litres of water per person per day (3 days supply)
🍱 FOOD biscuits, dry fruit, canned items
🔦Flashlight + extra batteries
🩹First aid kit with basic medicines and bandages
📄Copies of CNIC, property papers, documents
📱Fully charged power bank
💊Prescription medicines (7 day supply)
🧥Warm clothes + rain gear for each person
💰Emergency cash (ATMs may not work)
🪣Plastic bags, rope, whistle, basic tools
👶Baby supplies if needed — milk, diapers
